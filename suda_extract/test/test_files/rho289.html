Headword: *(ruka/na
Adler number: rho,289
Translated headword: plane
Vetting Status: high
Translation:
A woodcarving tool.
"And an axe, and a bright plane, and a rounded drill."[1]
Plane [is] feminine.
Greek Original:
*(ruka/na: tektoniko\n e)rgalei=on. kai\ pe/lekun, r(uka/nan t' eu)age/a, kai\ periage\s tru/panon. qhlukw=s h( r(uka/na.
Note:
[1] Greek Anthology 6.204.3-4 (Leonidas of Tarentum), a carpenter retiring from his trade dedicates tools to Athena; cf. Gow and Page (vol. I, 109), (vol. II, 316), and other extracts from this epigram at delta 108 and pi 2298. Gow and Page find (ibid.) the rendering of eu)age/a (bright, clear, conspicuous; cf. LSJ s.v. (web address 1)) into English as bright (cf. Paton (404-405)) to be "plainly unsuitable". Although it is quite plausible that the sharpened blade of a wood plane would indeed be shiny and bright on its cutting edge, Gow and Page endorse (ibid.) such emendations as eu)a/kea (e.g. eu)h/kea, sharp-edged), eu)page/a (Doric dialect, well-constructed), and eu)axe/a (well-sounding).
